## 1. Layout essentials for covers and posters

- **Hero focal point**: model or product as the visual anchor—avoid clutter.
- **Title safe zone**: reserve space for masthead and headlines.
- **Series consistency**: lock palette and lighting keywords across a set.

## 2. Prompt structure

Use **scene + fashion photography style + lighting + composition (negative space) + quality**. Example:

```text
Fashion magazine cover layout, editorial photography, negative space for headline, soft studio lighting, elegant pose, high-end styling, 8k, color grading cinematic
```

| Module | Role | Tip |
|--------|------|-----|
| Negative space | Room for titles | negative space, clean background area |
| Lighting | Premium feel | softbox, rim light, subtle contrast |
| Style | Magazine look | editorial, luxury campaign |

## 3. Production flow

1. Choose **aspect ratio** for the channel (e.g. 3:4 or 4:5 for vertical covers).
2. Lock composition, then refine color—avoid rewriting the whole prompt.
3. Add typography in post; AI stage focuses on a **clean hero visual**.

## 4. Conclusion

Fashion covers need **hero + negative space + light**. On **LimaxAI**, iterate fast and pick the best frame for layout and publishing.
