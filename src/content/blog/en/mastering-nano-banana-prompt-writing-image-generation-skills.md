## The Anatomy of a Perfect "Nano Banana" Prompt

Creating this specific look isn't magic; it's a formula. To get that premium "vinyl toy" look, your prompt needs to hit four specific pillars: **Subject, Material, Lighting, and Renderer.**

### 1. The Subject: Think "Chibi"
The Nano Banana style thrives on exaggerated proportions. You aren't looking for realism; you are looking for *appeal*.
* **Keywords to use:** `Chibi`, `Cute`, `Big head`, `Small body`, `Expressive eyes`, `Round forms`.

### 2. The Material: It Must Be Tactile
This is where 90% of beginners fail. If you don't define the texture, the AI will give you a generic cartoon. You want it to look like a physical object you can touch.
* **Keywords to use:** `PVC texture`, `Clay material`, `Matte finish`, `Frosted glass`, `Glossy plastic`, `Vinyl toy`.

### 3. The Lighting: Studio Quality
Bad lighting kills 3D art. For that clean, high-end commercial look, you need soft, controlled lighting.
* **Keywords to use:** `Soft studio lighting`, `Rim light`, `Global illumination`, `Soft focus`, `Clean background`.

### 4. The Renderer: Fake It 'Til You Make It
Tell the AI which rendering engine it should mimic.
* **Keywords to use:** `C4D`, `Octane Render`, `Unreal Engine 5`, `Blender 3D`, `High fidelity`, `8k resolution`.

## Advanced Image Generation Skills: Lighting is Key

If your images still feel "flat," the secret ingredient is usually **Rim Lighting**.

Rim lighting (or backlighting) adds a glowing outline to your character, separating them from the background and adding depth.

* **Try adding this to your prompt:** `"Volumetric lighting, rim light, bloom effect"`
