## 1. Core capabilities and use cases

- **Camera and pacing**: Describe moves (push, pull, pan, follow, orbit) for clearer storytelling.
- **Consistency**: Iterate on the same prompt chain to reduce random jumps between frames.
- **Use cases**: Short openers, product promos, tutorial clips, social ads.

## 2. Prompt structure for camera control

Use **subject + scene + camera move + lighting + pacing feel + quality**. Example:

```text
Slow push-in, cinematic tracking shot, golden hour lighting, shallow depth of field, 24fps feel, smooth camera motion, 4K, film grain subtle
```

| Dimension | Focus | Tip |
|-----------|--------|-----|
| Camera move | Verbs like push / pan / orbit | Pick one primary move first |
| Light & mood | sunset / night / soft light | Lock keywords when brand tone is fixed |
| Format & pacing | vertical vs horizontal | Match the platform to reduce cropping |

## 3. Tips and pitfalls

- Validate motion and pacing with **low-cost drafts** before pushing resolution.
- Avoid conflicting moves in one prompt; **one primary move** is more stable.
- For consistent characters or products, strengthen **subject description** and **camera distance**.
